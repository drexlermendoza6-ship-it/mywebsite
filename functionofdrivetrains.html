ADVANTAGE AND DISADVANTAGE


< FRONT WHEEL DRIVE (FWD) ADVANTAGE:
FUEL EFFICIENCY: FWD vehicles are known for their fuel efficiency. Since there is no need to connect the front axles to the rear wheels, the drive axles tend to be shorter, which makes for a lighter vehicle. This equates to less power required and lower fuel consumption for the car.

LOWER COST: FWD drivetrains are less complicated for manufacturers, making them less expensive to produce. On the user’s side, this makes vehicles more affordable and easier to maintain. Excellent Performance in Different Weather: FWD vehicles maintain good traction on various road surfaces. They respond well to wet conditions, snow, gravel, sand, and more. Further, they have a lower tendency to ‘fishtail’ on low-friction surfaces.

FRONT WHEEL DRIVE (FWD) DISADVANTAGE:
FWD is generally less suited for: High-performance driving and racing, where RWD or AWD offers better handling and weight balance.
Heavy-duty towing, as the weight transfer during acceleration reduces grip on the front wheels.
Severe off-road conditions, which require the ruggedness and specialized gearing of a 4WD system.
