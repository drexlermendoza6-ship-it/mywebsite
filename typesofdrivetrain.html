TYPES OF DRIVETRAIN

FRONT-WHEEL DRIVE (FWD)
is a drivetrain where the engine's power is sent only to the front wheels, which are responsible for both steering and propulsion. This design provides good traction and interior space.
REAR-WHEEL DRIVE (RWD)
is a system where the engine's power is sent to and drives the rear wheels only, while the front wheels are responsible for steering. This provides better weight distribution and handling.
ALL-WHEEL DRIVE (AWD)
is a vehicle system that sends engine power to all four wheels simultaneously or as needed, providing enhanced traction and stability in various driving conditions.
FOUR WHEEL DRIVE (4X4)
is a system that sends power to all four wheels of a vehicle to provide increased traction, especially on off-road terrain, making it ideal for rugged environments.
