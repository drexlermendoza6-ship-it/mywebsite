TYPES OF DRIVETRAIN

FRONT-WHEEL DRIVE (FWD)
is a drivetrain where the engine's power is sent only to the front wheels, which are responsible for both steering and propelling the vehicle forward.

REAR-WHEEL DRIVE (RWD)
is a system where the engine's power is sent to and drives the rear wheels only, while the front wheels are responsible for steering.
ALl-WHEEL DRIVE (AWD)
is a vehicle system that sends engine power to all four wheels simultaneously or as needed, providing enhanced traction and stability, especially on slippery surfaces like rain, snow, or ice, by distributing torque across all tires for better grip and handling than 2WD systems.
FOUR WHEEL DRIVE (4X4)
is a system that sends power to all four wheels of a vehicle to provide increased traction, especially on off-road terrain, mud, sand, or snow. The power distribution can be manual, requiring the driver to engage it, or automatic depending on the system, and is often found in vehicles built for rougher conditions.
